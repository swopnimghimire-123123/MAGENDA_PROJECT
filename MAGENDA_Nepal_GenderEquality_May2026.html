Why Nepal Needs Bold Action Now

Nepal has made remarkable constitutional progress — 33% women's representation quota, criminalisation of Chhaupadi, and landmark domestic violence laws. Yet deep structural inequalities persist across caste, ethnicity, geography, and socioeconomic lines.

As student ambassadors, we are uniquely positioned to bridge the gap between policy and lived reality — using creativity, peer networks, and digital tools to spark real change.

Category 01

Digital & Social Media Campaigns

🎬
#SheLeadsNepal Reels Challenge
A coordinated Instagram and TikTok challenge where students share 30-second stories of women leaders in their communities — teachers, farmers, coders, activists. Each reel ends with a call to nominate the next woman.
Viral potential Low cost Youth-led
🗺️
Digital Safety Map for Women
Build a crowdsourced Google Maps layer tagging safe spaces, harassment hotspots, legal aid centres, and women's shelters in Kathmandu, Pokhara, and Biratnagar. Shared via QR codes at bus parks and universities.
Tech-based High impact
🎙️
Podcast: "Awaz Utha" (Raise Your Voice)
A student-produced Nepali-language podcast series featuring interviews with women from different castes, professions, and regions. Distributed through Spotify and WhatsApp audio groups for wider rural reach.
Multilingual Rural reach

Category 02

Arts, Culture & Street Action

🎪
Nukkad Natak Troupe
A travelling street theatre group performing short plays on dowry violence, Chhaupadi, and child marriage at local bazaars, bus parks, and community gatherings — using humour and drama to reach non-literate audiences.
Traditional format Non-literate reach
🖌️
Mural Project: "Shakti Walls"
Partnering with local artists to paint large-scale murals depicting powerful women figures — Sita, Durga, Bhrikuti, and contemporary Nepali women leaders — in high-traffic urban walls. Each mural carries a QR code linking to resources.
Permanent visibility Community art
📸
Photo Documentary: "Faces of Change"
A travelling photo exhibition capturing real women from across Nepal's 7 provinces — their work, their struggles, their joys. Exhibited in university campuses, libraries, and digital galleries. Submitted to UN Women's photo competitions.
Storytelling Province-wide

🏔️ Nepal-Specific Focus: The Issues That Demand Our Voice

Chhaupadi pratha, Deuki/Badi practices, Kamlari bonded labour, acid attacks, and trafficking disproportionately affect Nepal's most marginalised women. Our activities must name these explicitly — not sanitise them — while centering survivor voices and community solutions.

Category 03

Education & Skill-Building

💻
Girls in Tech Bootcamp
Weekend coding, app-building, and digital literacy workshops specifically for girls from government schools in Kathmandu Valley and Terai. Mentored by female tech professionals at KUSOM and partnered with Leapfrog Technology.
Skills-based Long-term impact
⚖️
Legal Literacy Camps
Plain-language workshops explaining Nepal's Gender Equality Act, Domestic Violence Act, and Safe Abortion Act to women in rural wards. Delivered in Nepali, Maithili, and Tamang languages with visual flip-charts.
Rights-based Multilingual Rural focus
🌸
Menstrual Health School Kit Drive
Distribute reusable pad kits and hygiene education packets to girls in 30 schools in Far-Western and Karnali provinces — the regions most affected by Chhaupadi. Pair with school-based discussion circles led by female health workers.
Chhaupadi focus Health-based

Foundation

Our Four Strategic Pillars

01

Reach the Unreached

Every activity must have a rural, low-literacy, and low-resource pathway. Nepal's gender inequality is sharpest in Karnali, Madhesh, and Sudurpashchim — not in Kathmandu. Radio, vernacular languages, visual media, and community health workers are our delivery channels beyond cities.

Partner with FM stations, FCHV networks, and ward offices in 5 targeted municipalities
02

Centre Marginalised Voices

Dalit women, Janajati women, women with disabilities, and single women face compounding discrimination. Our activities must not default to middle-class, Bahun-Chhetri narratives of womanhood. Co-design activities with women from these communities — don't design for them.

Involve Dalit and Janajati women's groups as co-facilitators, not just beneficiaries
03

Measure What We Do

Awareness without accountability is noise. Every activity should have a simple measure: number reached, attitudes surveyed before and after, follow-up actions taken. Use Google Forms, WhatsApp polls, and attendance counts. Report results at the next monthly meeting.

Develop a one-page M&E template for all activities by June 2026
04

Build for Sustainability

Student ambassador cohorts change every year. Every activity must leave behind a community asset — a trained Gender Champion, a resource library, a mural, a saved podcast — that outlasts our tenure. Institutionalise what works through university policy and municipality budgets.

Create a MAGENDA Resource Repository hosted on KU's intranet and shared publicly
